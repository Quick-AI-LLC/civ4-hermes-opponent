# Delaying Action / Holding Pattern — Bait with Obsolete Units

## When to Use

The enemy has just unlocked a unit class 10+ strength above your front-line troops AND Nick tells you his reinforcements are N turns away. You hold a key chokepoint city that blocks access to your interior.

**Session evidence:** Turn 435→437, Germany unlocked SAM Infantry (strength 18) vs our Macemen (strength 8). Nick's 8 Modern Armor were 3 turns out. Tadmekka (50,21) was the chokepoint.

## Execution

1. **Mass 15-20 obsolete units at the chokepoint.** Macemen are ideal — they lose every 1v1 vs SAM Infantry, but 5 macemen can take out 1 SAM through accumulated damage-per-combat.

2. **Do NOT reinforce with better units.** Keep riflemen/grenadiers/infantry at interior and southern cities. The obsolete units are MEAT — your good units surviving to fight alongside the reinforcements is the goal.

3. **Pull scattered survivors back in each turn.** After combat resolution, survivors scatter to nearby tiles. Teleport them back to the chokepoint immediately. Every extra maceman that delays the enemy one more turn is worth more than a fresh one.

4. **Mass Infantry/current-best-unit production in all interior cities.** By the time the meat shield collapses, fresh units should be rolling off the line.

5. **When reinforcements arrive, counter-punch immediately.** The holding pattern ends the moment Nick's army is in position — transition to offensive deployment that same turn.

## Garrison Math (Proven)

| Turn | Force at Tadmekka | German attackers | Outcome |
|------|-------------------|------------------|---------|
| 435 | 20 macemen | SAM Infantry stack | Held, 14 survivors |
| 436 | 14 macemen | SAM Infantry stack | Held, 8 survivors |
| 437 | 8 macemen | — | Modern Armor arrived, relief |

Without the speed bump, Gao (5 tiles NW) and Tekedda (5 tiles W) would have fallen. The 20 macemen cost ~60 gold/turn maintenance but saved cities worth thousands of hammers.

## Common Mistakes

- **Stripping southern defense to feed the meat grinder.** If you pull Tekedda's 12 garrison into Tadmekka, England walks in the back door. The whole point is to hold with what you have there — don't cannibalize other fronts.
- **Reinforcing with your best units.** Sending Infantry to die alongside macemen wastes production. Let the obsolete units do their job.
- **Abandoning the city.** If you leave Tadmekka empty, the enemy takes it for free and your interior cities are next. Even 1 maceman on the tile forces combat resolution and delays them.
